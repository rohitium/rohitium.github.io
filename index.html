About Me

Hello! I'm a research scientist with a specialization in biophysics. I enjoy working on projects that intersect natural sciences, machine learning, and software development.

I have more than 10 years of research experience across industry and academia. Most recently, I was a Computational Biologist at Pivotal Life Sciences, an early-stage therapeutics-focused biotech Venture Capital (VC) firm. There, I was part of the AI and Data Intelligence team, building an internal AI platform for due dilligence and NewCo creation.

Previously, I was a Multiscale Modeling Researcher at Genentech, where I developed a computational workflow for predicting protein-protein and protein-drug interaction kinetics using Alphafold, Molecular Dynamics simulations, Markov State Models, and analytical rate theories from statistical mechanics. I also collaborated with Discovery Chemistry and PK/PD teams to generate molecular-level insights into portfolio drug candidates.

I did my postdoc at the University of California, Berkeley, in the lab of Carlos J Bustamante, where I worked on interrogating dynamics of artificially designed proteins using optical tweezers experiments. I also contributed to an explanation for anomalous power spectra of DNA Origami tethers in these experiments.

I earned my PhD from the University of Texas at Austin, in the group of Dmitrii E Makarov, where I contributed to the development of a theory for interpreting dynamics of low-dimensional coordinates observed in single-molecule experiments and simulations. Briefly, we tested non-Markovian models, such as the Generalized Langevin Equation, Generalized Smoluchowski Equation, and Fractional Brownian Motion, and found that these models where unable to explain the long tails observed in transition path time distributions.

Serendipitously, we found that there is a theoretical bound on the width of transition path time distributions for Markovian models, and this bound is violated by the observed long tails. This implied that the use of the one-dimensional diffusion model to interpret dynamics of protein folding in single molecule experiments is unjustified.

I am originally from New Delhi, India, and I currently live in the SF Bay Area, CA. I like playing tennis and chess, reading philosophy, and going on hikes with my wife and dog.
